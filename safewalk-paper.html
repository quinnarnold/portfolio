When Spatial Statistics Outperform Machine Learning: Corridor-Based Ground-Truth Evaluation of Crime Risk Prediction for Pedestrian Safety Routing

Quinn Arnold · Submitted to Journal of Transport Geography (Elsevier) · 2026

KDE achieves 54.5% greater crime avoidance than XGBoost and 53.3% greater than GNN across 36,000 route evaluations per method on 1.15 million street segments in Chicago. The performance hierarchy — KDE > GNN ≈ XGBoost > Getis-Ord Gi* > Historical Average — is consistent across all robustness checks.
