SafeWalk — Pedestrian Safety Routing Platform

Submitted to Journal of Transport Geography (Elsevier) · 2026

Full-stack safety navigation platform combining spatial statistics research with a production cloud backend and iOS application. Built the routing engine, data pipeline, risk-scoring models, and mobile client. Research paper demonstrates KDE achieves 54.5% greater crime avoidance than XGBoost and 53.3% greater than GNN for pedestrian routing on 1.15M street segments in Chicago.

Adaptive Patrol Route Optimization

Targeting journal submission and department pilot

Dynamic patrol routing system that generates shift-aware routes optimized for crime deterrence using continuously updated spatial risk surfaces. Evaluates multiple routing strategies against real holdout crime data across a major U.S. city, with a fleet coordination layer handling multi-officer dispatch and mid-patrol reallocation. Currently preparing for controlled pilot deployment with a police department.

Novel architecture for spatial optimization under prediction-decision misalignment

Novel architecture that bridges the gap between prediction accuracy and downstream decision quality for spatial optimization tasks. Explores decision-focused learning where the model optimizes directly for routing outcomes rather than crime count prediction, addressing the predict-then-optimize misalignment identified in the SafeWalk paper.
